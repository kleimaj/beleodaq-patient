BELEODAQ is a prescription medicine used to treat adults with a type of cancer called peripheral T-cell lymphoma (PTCL) that has come back (relapsed) or does not respond to previous treatment (refractory).1

BELEODAQ may be an option for you

Your doctor may have talked with you about treatment with BELEODAQ, and you may be looking for more information to see if it is right for you. Or you may be researching possible treatment options on your own.

This website will tell you about how BELEODAQ works, what it may be like to take BELEODAQ, and what to expect as you go through treatment. Here are a few of the main reasons to consider BELEODAQ:

BELEODAQ has helped patients with relapsed or refractory PTCL

BELEODAQ is an FDA-approved, single-agent treatment that has been shown to be effective in stopping cancer cells from growing in relapsed or refractory PTCL.1

Learn more about the clinical trial results for BELEODAQ

BELEODAQ is well tolerated

In clinical studies, most patients who received BELEODAQ had side effects that were generally mild to moderate in severity. The most common side effects were nausea, fatigue, fever, anemia, and vomiting.1,2

Learn more about the possible side effects with BELEODAQ

BELEODAQ may fit your daily routine

BELEODAQ is given in a 3-week cycle that includes 5 consecutive days of active treatment with a 30-minute intravenous (IV) infusion each day, followed by 16 days off treatment. With a short, 30-minute infusion on days 1 through 5, you may have time to fit additional activities that are important to you into your day.1
