IS BELEODAQ RIGHT FOR YOU? CLINICAL TRIAL RESULTS

Is BELEODAQ right for you?

That’s a question to discuss with your doctor when you review your treatment options.

It may help to learn who took part in a clinical trial that studied BELEODAQ in 129 patients with relapsed or refractory PTCL.2

The trial included patients aged 29 to 81.2

  • 48% of patients were 65 years or older1
  • 52% of patients were younger than 65 years1

Everyone in the study had tried other PTCL treatments that were no longer working.1,2

21% of patients had a stem cell transplant (SCT) before getting BELEODAQ, and 7.5% received an SCT after treatment with BELEODAQ.1,2

The trial included patients with the most common subtypes of PTCL.1,4

BELEODAQ has been shown to be effective against the most common subtypes of PTCL2,4

  • Peripheral T-cell lymphoma not otherwise speciﬁed (PTCL-NOS)
  • Angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma (AITL)
  • Anaplastic large cell lymphoma (ALCL)

BELEODAQ is well tolerated1,2

In clinical trials, most patients had side effects that were generally mild to moderate in severity. The most common side effects were nausea, fatigue, fever, anemia, and vomiting.

Please see the Important Safety Information and Full Prescribing Information to review the possible side effects of BELEODAQ.

All patients received 30-minute infusions of BELEODAQ once a day for 5 days. Every 3 weeks, a new cycle of daily infusions on days 1 through 5, followed by 16 days off treatment, could be repeated.1

BELEODAQ offers dosing that may give you time to do more with your day1

  • With a 30-minute IV infusion on days 1 through 5 of a 3-week cycle, you’ll have time to fit additional activities that are important to you into your day.
