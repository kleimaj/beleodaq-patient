Coping with treatment

When cancer returns, it’s normal to have fears and concerns10

You have decisions to make, feelings to sort out, and uncertainty about what comes next. Talking with your doctor and family members about your needs, wishes, and priorities can help you choose a way forward that lets you live as well as you can for as long as possible.

Talking with your doctor can ease your concerns and help you understand your options. Asking questions can help your doctor suggest a treatment that’s a good fit for you. In addition, taking an active role in your care can give you a greater sense of control as you go through treatment.
