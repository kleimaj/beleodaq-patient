Coping with treatment

When cancer returns, it’s normal to have fears and concerns10

You have decisions to make, feelings to sort out, and uncertainty about what comes next. Talking with your doctor and family members about your needs, wishes, and priorities can help you choose a way forward that lets you live as well as you can for as long as possible.

Talking with your doctor can ease your concerns and help you understand your options. Asking questions can help your doctor suggest a treatment that’s a good fit for you. In addition, taking an active role in your care can give you a greater sense of control as you go through treatment.

As you weigh your options, it’s important to think about how to balance the demands of treatment.11

These tips can help you get the support you need to make more of each day as you go through treatment.11

  • Ask your healthcare team how to manage the side effects of your cancer treatment.
  • Learn to accept help when it’s offered, but speak up if you’re not getting the help you need.
  • Pay attention to the small things that give you comfort and joy and try to build them into your day.
  • If you want to keep up your usual routine, accept that you may need to rest when you feel tired and adjust your schedule on days you don’t feel well.
  • Keep up with exercise, hobbies, games, and other activities that help you stay strong mentally and physically. Always check with your doctor before starting a new exercise program.
  • If you will be working while in treatment, stay in touch with your employer and coworkers to let them know how you’re doing. Speak up if you need ﬂexible hours, time off, or help to finish tasks and meet deadlines.
  • Make an effort to spend time with people who make you happy or who you feel comfortable around.
  • If you are having a hard time, find someone to talk with who understands what you are going through.
